# Nitaqat (نطاقات)

## Short definition

**Nitaqat** is Saudi Arabia's Saudization program that classifies private-sector establishments by how effectively they employ Saudi nationals, using color-coded bands (e.g., platinum, green, yellow, red).

## Purpose

The program incentivizes companies to increase Saudi employment, improve workforce nationalization, and align hiring with labor-market policy goals. Classification can affect access to government services, visa issuance, and compliance standing.

## Key services and concepts

- **Color bands** — performance tiers reflecting Saudization ratios and compliance
- **Establishment classification** — periodic evaluation of Saudi vs. non-Saudi workforce mix
- **Compliance incentives** — benefits for higher tiers; restrictions for lower tiers
- **Qiwa linkage** — Nitaqat status is visible and managed through Qiwa for many employers
- **Labor policy alignment** — supports Vision 2030 human-capital and employment goals

## Why it matters for ArabArenaAI

Business and government benchmark items often mention Nitaqat, Saudization, or "green/platinum" status. Models must understand it as a workforce compliance framework—not a tax system or a separate company.
