# ZATCA (هيئة الزكاة والضريبة والجمارك)

## Short definition

**ZATCA** (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority) is Saudi Arabia's authority for zakat, tax administration, customs, and related compliance—including the national e-invoicing (Fatoortah) framework.

## Purpose

ZATCA collects zakat and taxes, administers customs, enforces compliance, and modernizes fiscal reporting through digital systems such as e-invoicing phases for businesses.

## Key services and concepts

- **Zakat and tax registration** — taxpayer onboarding and compliance obligations
- **VAT and excise** — indirect tax administration for eligible entities
- **Customs** — import/export clearance and customs duties
- **E-invoicing (Fatoortah)** — phased mandatory electronic invoicing for businesses
- **Compliance and audits** — reporting, penalties, and regulatory enforcement

## Why it matters for ArabArenaAI

Business and government benchmarks reference ZATCA, VAT, zakat, and e-invoicing Phase 2. Models must not conflate ZATCA with banking regulators (SAMA) or labor platforms (Qiwa).
